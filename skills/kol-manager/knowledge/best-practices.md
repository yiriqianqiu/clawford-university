# Crypto KOL Campaign — Best Practices

## KOL Selection Process

### Step 1: Define Your Ideal KOL Profile
Before searching for KOLs, define your requirements:
- **Chain alignment**: Does the KOL cover your chain ecosystem (ETH, Solana, BSC, Base, etc.)?
- **Vertical alignment**: Does the KOL focus on your vertical (DeFi, NFTs, GameFi, infra, memecoins)?
- **Audience overlap**: Does the KOL's audience match your target user persona?
- **Content quality**: Does the KOL produce thoughtful analysis or low-effort shills?
- **Reputation**: Has the KOL been associated with rugged projects or scams?

### Step 2: Build a Longlist
- Source from KOL databases: Kaito, LunarCrush, DWF Ventures KOL list, ICO Analytics influencer tracker
- Monitor who is already talking about your vertical or competitors
- Ask your existing community: "Which voices do you trust in [your vertical]?"
- Track who speaks at major conferences (Token2049, ETH Denver, Consensus)

### Step 3: Score and Shortlist
Rate each KOL on a 1–5 scale across:
| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Relevance | 30% | How closely does their content match your project? |
| Authenticity | 25% | Engagement quality, follower legitimacy, promotion history |
| Reach | 20% | Total audience size across platforms |
| Engagement | 15% | Average engagement rate relative to follower count |
| Value | 10% | Cost relative to expected reach and engagement |

### Step 4: Deep-Vet the Shortlist
For each shortlisted KOL:
- Review their last 30 days of posts — how many projects did they promote?
- Check if promoted projects are still alive or rugged
- Run fake-follower analysis using tools from knowledge/domain.md
- Verify they have real influence by checking if their posts generate genuine discussion (not just likes)
- Check for conflicts: Are they invested in or advising a competitor?

## Negotiation Best Practices

### Pricing Anchors
- Never accept the KOL's first price. Most inflate by 50–100% expecting negotiation.
- Research market rates for their tier before engaging. Use industry benchmarks:
  - Nano: $50–$500/post
  - Micro: $500–$3,000/post
  - Macro: $3,000–$15,000/post
  - Mega: $10,000–$100,000+/post
- Counter with data: "Based on your average engagement rate of X% and follower count of Y, the market rate for similar KOLs is $Z."

### Contract Structure
Every KOL engagement should have a written agreement covering:
1. **Deliverables**: Exact number, type, and format of posts/videos/spaces
2. **Timeline**: Specific dates and times for each deliverable
3. **Content approval**: Project has right to review content before publication (24-hour review window)
4. **Disclosure**: KOL must comply with FTC/local advertising disclosure requirements
5. **Exclusivity window**: KOL will not promote direct competitors within X days before/after
6. **Payment terms**: Milestone-based with final payment after all deliverables confirmed
7. **Performance clause**: Bonus/penalty tied to agreed KPIs
8. **Revision policy**: 1 revision round included; additional revisions at $X each
9. **Kill clause**: Either party can cancel with 48-hour notice before campaign start; partial payment for completed work
10. **IP and usage rights**: Project can repurpose KOL content in its own marketing

### Payment Structuring
- **Upfront**: Max 30% of total fee before first deliverable
- **Milestone**: 40% after core deliverables confirmed live
- **Completion**: 30% after all deliverables verified and performance window closes
- **Bonus**: Additional 10–20% if KPIs exceeded by >50%
- **Token payments**: Always vest. Minimum 3-month cliff, 6–12 month linear vest. Include clawback if deliverables not met.

### Verification Checklist Per Deliverable
- [ ] Post is live and matches approved content
- [ ] Proper disclosure included (#ad, #sponsored, or equivalent)
- [ ] Post timing matches the agreed schedule
- [ ] No competing project promotions within exclusivity window
- [ ] Engagement is organic (spot-check replies for quality)
- [ ] Referral/tracking links are correctly embedded
- [ ] Screenshot captured for records (KOLs sometimes delete posts)

### Response Protocol
| Issue | Response | Timeline |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Late delivery (< 24 hours) | Send reminder, note in tracking | Immediate |
| Late delivery (> 48 hours) | Escalate, consider withholding milestone payment | Within 72 hours |
| Content deviation from brief | Request revision, invoke revision clause | Within 4 hours of detection |
| Fake engagement detected | Pause payments, document evidence, demand explanation | Within 24 hours |
| KOL promotes competitor during exclusivity | Invoke contract clause, reduce final payment | Within 24 hours |

## Multi-Platform Campaign Coordination

### Campaign Wave Strategy
Structure campaigns in waves, not all at once:

**Wave 1 — Seed (Days 1–3)**
- 2–3 micro KOLs post first to establish organic buzz
- Focus on thoughtful threads and analysis
- Goal: Create the "people are talking about this" effect

**Wave 2 — Amplify (Days 4–7)**
- 1–2 macro KOLs amplify with broader reach
- Time posts to coincide with project milestones (launch, snapshot, listing)
- Include a Twitter Space or AMA for interactive engagement

**Wave 3 — Sustain (Days 8–14)**
- Ambassador program kicks in with nano KOLs posting regularly
- Meme campaign layer for cultural reinforcement
- Community-generated content incentivized via contests

**Wave 4 — Reinforce (Days 15–30)**
- YouTube long-form reviews go live (produced during Wave 1–2)
- Follow-up threads from Wave 1 KOLs with updated metrics
- Case study / "results so far" content

### Cross-Platform Synergy
- A KOL posts a thread on X → clip a highlight for Telegram channels → reference in Discord AMA
- YouTube video → excerpt as Twitter clip → full link shared in Telegram
- Twitter Space recording → distribute as podcast clip → summarize as thread
- Every piece of content should exist in at least 2 platform formats

## Community Sentiment Monitoring

### Monitoring Setup
- Track project mentions across X, Telegram, Discord, Reddit, and CT aggregators
- Categorize sentiment: positive, neutral, negative, FUD, shill
- Monitor sentiment ratio: healthy is >50% positive, <15% negative
- Set alerts for sentiment ratio drops below 40% positive

### Responding to Negative Sentiment
| Sentiment Type | Response Strategy |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Legitimate criticism | Acknowledge, address factually, show improvement plan |
| Misinformed FUD | Provide data and facts; recruit community advocates to amplify truth |
| Coordinated attack | Document, report, prepare counter-narrative, alert community mods |
| KOL backlash | If justified, pause campaign and reassess. If unjustified, provide evidence to community. |
| Scam accusations | Immediate transparent response with on-chain proof, audit links, team doxxing |
