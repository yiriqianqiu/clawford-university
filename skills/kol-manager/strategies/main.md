# KOL Campaign Strategy

## Step 1: Define Campaign Goals and Context
- Parse the user's request to identify: **campaign objective**, **project details**, **target audience**, **budget range**, **timeline**, **chain/ecosystem**
- Classify the campaign objective:
  - **Awareness**: Maximize reach and brand recognition (new project, new chain)
  - **Community Growth**: Grow Discord/Telegram/Twitter following with engaged members
  - **Token Launch (TGE)**: Drive participation in token generation event or initial liquidity
  - **Exchange Listing**: Amplify listing announcement, drive trading volume
  - **TVL Growth**: Attract depositors to DeFi protocol
  - **NFT Mint**: Drive mint participation and secondary market interest
  - **Damage Control**: Counter FUD, rebuild trust after incident
- Assess project maturity:
  - Pre-launch: No live product → focus on narrative and anticipation
  - Early-stage: Live product, small community → focus on organic growth and credibility
  - Growth-stage: Established product, seeking scale → focus on reach and conversion
- OUTPUT: Campaign brief with objective, audience profile, budget, timeline, and success criteria

## Step 2: Identify and Classify Target KOLs
- Based on the campaign brief, define the ideal KOL profile using knowledge/domain.md tier classifications
- Determine optimal tier mix based on campaign goal:
  - Awareness: Heavy macro + mega, supported by micro
  - Community growth: Heavy micro + nano, supported by macro
  - Token launch: Balanced mix across all tiers, timed in waves
  - Exchange listing: Heavy macro + mega for maximum reach
- FOR EACH tier in the campaign:
  - Define minimum requirements: follower count range, engagement rate floor, niche alignment, platform presence
  - Specify deal-breakers: past rug promotions, competitor conflicts, fake follower thresholds
- Identify specific KOL archetypes to target:
  - **Alpha Hunters**: Share early finds, degen audience, high conversion
  - **Analyst/Researchers**: Deep dives, methodical audience, high trust
  - **Community Leaders**: Run large groups, organize events, strong loyalty
  - **Content Creators**: YouTube/video focused, production quality, broad reach
  - **Meme Lords**: Cultural influence, viral potential, hard to control messaging
- OUTPUT: KOL selection matrix with tier allocation, minimum criteria per tier, and archetype targets

## Step 3: Vet KOL Authenticity
- FOR EACH candidate KOL, run the vetting pipeline:
  1. **Quantitative check**: Apply fake follower detection signals from knowledge/domain.md
     - Engagement rate within tier benchmarks?
     - Like-to-reply ratio healthy (<50:1)?
     - Follower growth pattern organic (no sudden spikes)?
     - Audience geography consistent with content language?
  2. **Promotion history check**: Review last 90 days of promotional posts
     - How many projects promoted? (>10/month = red flag)
     - Fate of promoted projects? (>2 rugged = disqualify)
     - Does the KOL disclose paid partnerships?
  3. **Reputation check**: Search for the KOL's name in crypto media, complaint threads, and rug databases
     - Any scam associations?
     - Community sentiment toward this KOL?
     - Do respected accounts vouch for or criticize them?
  4. **Audience overlap check**: Estimate overlap between candidates
     - >30% overlap between two KOLs in same campaign = redundant, replace one
- Score each KOL: Relevance (30%) + Authenticity (25%) + Reach (20%) + Engagement (15%) + Value (10%)
- DISQUALIFY any KOL that fails the quantitative check or has >2 rug-associated promotions
- OUTPUT: Vetted KOL shortlist with scores, tier assignment, and recommended platform focus

## Step 4: Negotiate Terms and Structure Agreements
- FOR EACH selected KOL, prepare a negotiation package:
  1. **Research market rate** for their tier, platform, and deliverable type
  2. **Design compensation structure** using the hybrid model from knowledge/domain.md:
     - Upfront fee (30–50% of market rate)
     - Token allocation (vested 6–12 months with cliff)
     - Performance bonus tied to specific KPIs
  3. **Draft deliverable schedule**:
     - Exact content pieces, formats, and platforms
     - Publication dates and times
     - Content review window (24 hours before publication)
     - Exclusivity period (no competitor promotion X days before/after)
  4. **Define performance KPIs**:
     - Minimum engagement thresholds per post
     - Conversion targets (wallet connections, joins, etc.)
     - Bonus trigger thresholds
- Structure payment milestones per knowledge/best-practices.md:
  - 30% upfront on contract signing
  - 40% on delivery confirmation (all posts live and verified)
  - 30% after 7-day performance window closes
- VERIFY: Contract includes disclosure compliance, content approval, revision policy, kill clause
- OUTPUT: Per-KOL contract terms with deliverables, timeline, payment schedule, and KPIs

## Step 5: Execute Campaign in Waves
- Organize execution using the wave strategy from knowledge/best-practices.md:

### Wave 1 — Seed (Days 1–3)
- Deploy 2–3 micro KOLs with thoughtful, analytical content
- Focus on X threads and Telegram alpha group mentions
- Goal: Create organic-looking early buzz; establish the narrative
- MONITOR: Engagement rates, sentiment, any immediate red flags

### Wave 2 — Amplify (Days 4–7)
- Deploy 1–2 macro KOLs for broader reach
- Schedule a Twitter Space or AMA with the project team + KOLs
- Time posts to coincide with a project milestone (feature launch, partnership announcement, snapshot)
- Cross-post content across platforms per knowledge/best-practices.md synergy approach
- MONITOR: Follower growth trajectory, on-chain conversions, volume impact

### Wave 3 — Sustain (Days 8–14)
- Activate nano KOL ambassador program for sustained grassroots posting
- Launch meme campaign layer if relevant to project culture
- Incentivize community-generated content (contests, bounties)
- MONITOR: Sentiment trends, organic vs paid engagement ratio, holder retention

### Wave 4 — Reinforce (Days 15–30)
- YouTube long-form reviews go live
- Follow-up threads from Wave 1–2 KOLs with campaign results and updated metrics
- Community recap and celebration of growth milestones
- MONITOR: Long-tail conversion from YouTube, sustained holder/follower growth

- VERIFY against anti-patterns from knowledge/anti-patterns.md at each wave:
  - No simultaneous shill blast
  - Content matches briefs
  - Disclosures present
  - Engagement looks organic
- IF a wave underperforms kill criteria THEN pause, diagnose, and pivot before next wave

## Step 6: Measure ROI and Optimize
- After the campaign window closes, compile comprehensive ROI analysis:
  1. **Per-KOL Performance Report**:
     - Deliverables completed vs contracted
     - Impressions, engagement rate, reply quality
     - Attributable conversions (via referral links/codes)
     - Cost per acquisition (CPA)
     - Cost per follower
     - Qualitative assessment: Did their content drive genuine discussion?
  2. **Aggregate Campaign Report**:
     - Total reach and engagement across all KOLs
     - Net new followers retained (7-day and 30-day)
     - On-chain metrics: wallet connections, holder growth, TVL change, volume impact
     - Sentiment shift: pre vs post campaign
     - Total campaign cost vs attributed value
     - Overall ROI percentage
  3. **KOL Database Update**:
     - Record each KOL's performance for future reference
     - Flag top performers for future campaigns
     - Flag underperformers with specific issues (late delivery, fake engagement, etc.)
  4. **Optimization Recommendations**:
     - Which tiers and archetypes delivered the best ROI?
     - Which platforms drove the most conversions?
     - What messaging resonated vs fell flat?
     - Budget reallocation recommendations for next campaign
- OUTPUT: Structured campaign report with per-KOL scores, aggregate metrics, and actionable recommendations for the next campaign
